Research Insights

Key Finding: For inactive 60-year-olds, adding just 10 minutes of daily brisk walking was linked to 0.9-1.4 extra years of life.
Maximum Benefit: The least active Americans could gain nearly 11 years by increasing daily walking by 111 minutes.
Accessibility: Walking doesn't need to be intense to provide substantial longevity benefits.

Data sourced from British Journal of Sports Medicine and University of Leicester research studies
